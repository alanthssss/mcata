## Short Hook

## Core Gameplay Description

In **Merge Catalyst**, you slide tiles on a 4×4 grid to merge them and generate
Output. But this isn't 2048 — the real game is in the **Catalysts** you equip.

Each run, you build a loadout of up to 6 Catalysts that change how your merges
score: amplify chain reactions, recover energy from tiles, shield against
anomalies, or bend the rules of the board entirely. Reach the Output target
before your Steps run out — and survive 6 escalating phases to complete the run.

Between phases, visit the **Forge** to buy new Catalysts with Energy, or choose
an **Infusion** reward to boost your multiplier, steps, or resource pool.

Every run is a fresh build. Every build creates a different engine.

---

## Key Features

- **Catalyst Builds** — choose from 24 Catalysts across 5 categories (Amplifier, Stabilizer, Generator, Modifier, Legacy). Mix and match to find synergies.
- **Synergy System** — certain Catalyst pairs unlock powerful bonus multipliers when both are equipped.
- **Momentum** — consecutive scoring moves build a rolling multiplier up to ×2.0. Break the streak and it resets.
- **Anomaly Phases** — two late-game phases introduce board disruptions (Entropy Tax, Collapse Field) that demand adaptive play.
- **Protocols** — choose a run ruleset at the start (Corner Protocol, Sparse Protocol, Overload Protocol) to change the strategic landscape.
- **Signals** — one-use tactical abilities that activate on your next move.
- **Progression Tier Display** — tiles display progression labels (Seed → Iron → Gold → Diamond → Singularity) so the board reads as a build state, not a math problem.
- **Catalyst Collection** — track every Catalyst you've encountered, with rarity, tags, and flavor text.
- **Full Localization** — English and Chinese (Simplified) included, with i18n-ready architecture.
- **Benchmark AI** — a full AI benchmark suite for balance validation (not in the player build).

---

## Why It's Different

Most merge puzzle games stop at "get the big number." Merge Catalyst asks
*how* you get there and *what you built to do it*.

- No predetermined optimal strategy — each Catalyst combination rewards a
  different play style.
- Scoring isn't just tiles — chain length, board position, phase timing, and
  catalyst synergies all multiply together.
- The board state is a *build state*: you're managing resources, momentum,
  and risk simultaneously.
- Roguelike run structure means no two sessions are identical.
